How to Play ?

  • Players: Two players take turns. One player uses X, and the other uses O.
  • Starting the Game: One player is chosen to go first.
  • Making a Move: On each turn, a player marks one of the empty squares with their symbol (X or O).
  • Winning the Game: The first player to get three of of their symbols in a row horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
  • Ending in a Draw: If all nine squares are filled and neither player has three in a row, the game ends in a draw (also known as a tie or stalemate).

Additional Rules

  • No Overwriting: Once a player places their symbol in a square, it cannot be changed or moved.
  • Turns Alternate: Players alternate turns. If player X goes first, player O will go next, and so on.

Strategy Tips

  • Forking: Create opportunities where you have two ways to win on your next move.
  • Blocking: Always check if your opponent is about to win on their next move and block them if possible.
  • Center: Controlling the center square can give you more opportunities to create a row of three.
